    Y2K Problem v2 (Y2k38)

    All The 32-bit devices like old cell phones (made in 2010s and older) will not be able to work anymore

    Why will this happen?
    Most of the computers has a built-in clock in it, if you remove the clock then computer can't boot. You need to make a special Operating System for it to work without clock. The source of the problem starts at right here: These clocks are some sort of a mechanism that increments a number by 1/sec and it's start date is 1 Jan 1970 00:00:00 (when the number is 0) for example if the number is 100 then the date will be 1 Jan 1970 00:01:40. This clock has a limit and limit is 2³¹-1 = 2,147,483,647 which means 03:14:07 on Tuesday, 19 January 2038. When the clock arrives to it's limit, it goes to the smallest possible integer which is minus 2,147,483,648 for 32-bit clocks so the date will go back to 20:45:52 on Friday, 13 December 1901 and most of the programs will be crashed because of this problem.

    Lunar Crater Radio Telescope (LCRT) on the Far-Side of the Moon

    The telescope in Earth can be affected by us sometimes. Lights, radio traffic, pollution and etc. So NASA decided to make a radio telescope in Moon.
    "A radio telescope is a specialized antenna and radio receiver used to detect radio waves from astronomical radio sources in the sky."
    "An ultra-long-wavelength radio telescope on the far-side of the Moon has tremendous advantages compared to Earth-based and Earth-orbiting telescopes, including: (i) Such a telescope can observe the universe at wavelengths greater than 10m (i.e., frequencies below 30MHz), which are reflected by the Earth's ionosphere and are hitherto largely unexplored by humans, and (ii) the Moon acts as a physical shield that isolates the lunar-surface telescope from radio interferences/noises from Earth-based sources, ionosphere, Earth-orbiting satellites, and Sun’s radio-noise during the lunar night."

    (Source)
